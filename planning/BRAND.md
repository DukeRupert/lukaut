## Visual Identity

### Logo Direction

#### Concept
The logo should convey **watchfulness** and **protection** without being literal (no hard hats, warning signs, or cartoon eyes). It should feel modern and software-native while being appropriate for a professional B2B context.

#### Logo Approaches

**Option A: Wordmark Only**
A clean, custom wordmark with subtle distinctiveness in the letterforms. The "L" or "k" could have a subtle visual treatment suggesting vigilance or sight.

*Pros:* Simple, versatile, easy to reproduce
*Cons:* Less distinctive at small sizes

**Option B: Lettermark + Wordmark**
A stylized "L" mark that can stand alone as an icon, paired with the wordmark.

*Pros:* Flexible (icon for app, full logo for web), more distinctive
*Cons:* More complex to execute well

**Option C: Abstract Symbol + Wordmark**
A simple geometric symbol suggesting oversight, vision, or protectionâ€”paired with the wordmark.

*Pros:* Most distinctive, scalable
*Cons:* Risk of being too abstract or generic

**Recommendation:** Option B (Lettermark + Wordmark) â€” provides flexibility for app icon, favicon, and full logo usage while maintaining brand recognition.

#### Logo Principles

- **Simplicity:** Must work at 16x16px (favicon) and on a hard hat sticker
- **No literal imagery:** Avoid hard hats, clipboards, checkmarks, warning triangles
- **Geometric foundation:** Clean lines, intentional shapes
- **Single color capable:** Must work in single color for various applications

#### Logo Don'ts

- No gradients in primary mark
- No thin lines that disappear at small sizes
- No clip art or stock icon aesthetics
- No "tech startup" clichÃ©s (swooshes, orbits, abstract nodes)
- Avoid anything that feels like a "tech startup logo generator"

### Typography

#### Font Selection

**Primary Font: Inter**
- **Why:** Clean, highly readable, excellent at small sizes, extensive weight range, free and open source
- **Use for:** Headlines, body text, UI elements
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold)

**Monospace Font: JetBrains Mono** (optional)
- **Why:** Clean, readable monospace for any code or regulation citations
- **Use for:** OSHA regulation codes, technical references
- **Weights:** 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium)

#### Type Scale

| Element | Size | Weight | Line Height | Usage |
|---------|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| H1 | 36px / 2.25rem | 700 Bold | 1.2 | Page titles |
| H2 | 28px / 1.75rem | 600 SemiBold | 1.25 | Section headers |
| H3 | 22px / 1.375rem | 600 SemiBold | 1.3 | Subsection headers |
| H4 | 18px / 1.125rem | 500 Medium | 1.4 | Card headers |
| Body | 16px / 1rem | 400 Regular | 1.5 | Paragraphs, descriptions |
| Body Small | 14px / 0.875rem | 400 Regular | 1.5 | Secondary text, captions |
| Caption | 12px / 0.75rem | 500 Medium | 1.4 | Labels, metadata |
| Button | 14px / 0.875rem | 600 SemiBold | 1 | Button text |

#### Typography Guidelines

- **Headlines:** Sentence case ("Create a new inspection" not "Create A New Inspection")
- **Buttons:** Sentence case ("Generate report" not "GENERATE REPORT")
- **No all-caps** except for very short labels (e.g., "NEW", "BETA")
- **Maximum line length:** 65-75 characters for body text
